Noble price in chemistry

The Nobel Prize in Chemistry 2024 is about pro¬teins, life’s ingenious chemical tools. David Baker has succeeded with the almost impossible feat of building entirely new kinds of proteins. Demis Hassabis and John Jumper have developed an AI model to solve a 50-year-old problem: predicting proteins’ complex structures. These discoveries hold enormous potential.

Theoretical study on ligand conformational self-adaptation for modulating reactivity

The use of Josiphos-type ligands with Pd catalysts has led to efficient catalytic processes. Density functional theory (DFT) calculations reveal that the most favourable reaction pathway involves an unusual alkene insertion into the carbon-palladium bond, enhancing enantioselectivity. The self-adaptation of the Josiphos-Pd backbone allows for two distinct ligand conformations with different steric environments. A half-chair conformation is preferred for migratory insertion, which is both the rate-determining step and enantioselectivity controlling step. The steric effect between the phenyl group and the methyl group on the substrate is crucial for enantioselectivity control.

Theoretical Investigations of a point mutation affecting H5 Hemagglutinin’s receptor binding preference

The H5N1 virus, a subtype of influenza A, causes severe respiratory illness in birds and poses significant economic losses in poultry farming. The virus uses its surface glycoprotein, Hemagglutinin, to infect host cells. Studies show that a single amino acid substitution in Hemagglutinin can switch its glycan receptor preference from avian-type to human-type. This study aims to explain the mechanism of a mutation (D94N) on H5 HA, which alters the electrostatic interactions pattern near the HA receptor binding pocket, reducing stability for the HA-avian-type SAα2,3 Gal complex. However, the mutation's detrimental effect is not observed in the HA-human-type SAα2,6 Gal complex.

Upcoming Conferences and Workshops:

IUPAC 2025 aims to explore chemistry's role in addressing global sustainability challenges. The event includes scientific sessions on various chemistry disciplines and a special cluster on "Chemistry and Sustainable Development Goals" focusing on chemistry's solutions to SDGs. The 50th edition of the World Chemistry Congress will commemorate "100 years of Excellence in Chemistry Research and Innovations" and feature themed symposia on gender, diversity, equity, ethics, young chemists' networks, and emerging technologies.


ISTCP Congress 2024 The 11th triennial congress of the International Society of Theoretical Chemical Physics (ISTCP 2024) is set to provide an opportunity to stay updated on the latest scientific developments and develop new ideas. The conference will feature plenary speakers, invited talks, and poster presentations. The compact, central location and the invigorating sea breeze will stimulate the exchange of scientific ideas. The conference also offers tour options and a cable car or hike to Laoshan mountain.


WATOC 2025 Researchers in theoretical and computational chemistry are invited to submit abstracts for communications and posters. 150 slots will be selected by the Local Scientific Committee and International Advisory Board. Participants must complete their abstracts by March 1, 2025, and will be informed by May 16, 2025.
