Why Computational and Theoretical Chemistry?

Chemistry isn't just about beakers and test tubes; it's the key that unravels the mysteries of matter and its transformations. Within this vast field, Computational and Theoretical Chemistry stand out as pivotal disciplines that leverage mathematical models and computer simulations to understand chemical phenomena.

What Are They?

Theoretical Chemistry

Theoretical chemistry crafts the blueprints of molecular behaviour, using the laws of quantum mechanics and statistical mechanics. As Albert Einstein said, 'Imagination is more important than knowledge.' This field thrives on imagination, formulating models that explain the mysteries of chemical interactions. Its where abstract ideas take shape, offering insights into the fundamental laws that govern chemical reactions.

Computational Chemistry

Computational chemistry is where theory meets technology. It employs powerful simulations to analyse the complex chemical systems, turning abstract concepts into tangible insights. Think of it as a molecular crystal ball, revealing the future of chemical reactions
